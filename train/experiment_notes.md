## Important methodological note

Direct numerical comparison with `models/russian_traffic_signs.pt` is not valid.

Reason:

- `models/russian_traffic_signs.pt` contains `18` classes.
- The RTSD model was trained on `155` classes.
- Metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall and mAP must be calculated on the same label space.

Because of this, the report should present:

1. Quantitative metrics for the new RTSD-trained model.
2. Qualitative comparison with the original application model on several example images.
3. Explanation that the new model covers a much larger class space than the original model.

## Short text for the methods section

The training pipeline was implemented with the Ultralytics framework. The original RTSD annotations were converted from COCO-style JSON files into YOLO detection format, after which a custom dataset description file was generated. A pretrained YOLO12n detector was fine-tuned on the resulting dataset of Russian traffic signs. During training, mixed precision mode and cosine learning rate scheduling were used, while validation was performed automatically after each epoch. The final model was selected according to the best validation quality and saved as a `best.pt` checkpoint.

## Short text for the experiment section

An experiment was conducted to evaluate the quality of the trained traffic sign detector on the RTSD validation split. The model was trained on 54,188 images and validated on 5,000 images containing 155 traffic sign classes. The final training configuration used the YOLO12n architecture with an input resolution of 640x640 pixels, batch size 8 and 60 epochs. The model quality was assessed using Precision, Recall, mAP@0.50 and mAP@0.50:0.95 metrics. In addition, graphical learning curves, PR-curves and confusion matrices generated by Ultralytics were analyzed.

## Short text for the results section

The final trained model achieved a Precision of 0.766, Recall of 0.744, mAP@0.50 of 0.822 and mAP@0.50:0.95 of 0.611 on the validation set. These results indicate that the detector is able to reliably localize and classify Russian traffic signs under the conditions represented in the RTSD dataset. The obtained metrics, together with the validation examples and confusion matrix, confirm that the model is suitable for integration into the developed desktop MVP application for image, video and camera-stream analysis.
